International Law Commission

The International Law Commission is a body of experts responsible for helping develop and codify international law. It is composed of 34 individuals recognized for their expertise and qualifications in international law, who are elected by the UNGA every five years.

The ILC has since held annual sessions at the UN Office in Geneva to discuss and debate various topics in international law and develop international legal principles accordingly.
